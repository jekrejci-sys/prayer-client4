About the National
Day of Prayer

A sacred tradition rooted in America’s founding — now more vital than ever as our nation approaches its 250th year.

What Is the National Day of Prayer?

The National Day of Prayer is an annual observance held on the first Thursday of May, inviting people of all faiths to pray for the nation. Established by a joint resolution of Congress and signed into law by President Harry S. Truman in 1952, the National Day of Prayer has been a cornerstone of America’s civic and spiritual life for over seven decades.

The tradition of national prayer far predates even the 1952 law. The Continental Congress called for prayer during the Revolutionary War. President Abraham Lincoln proclaimed a day of fasting and prayer during the Civil War, acknowledging that the nation had “forgotten God” and needed His mercy. George Washington, John Adams, and James Madison all issued presidential proclamations calling the American people to prayer.

This 40-day campaign takes the spirit of the National Day of Prayer and extends it into a sustained, focused season of intercession — asking God to bless, heal, and guide our nation at a pivotal moment in history.

America’s 250th Anniversary

In 2026, the United States of America celebrates its 250th birthday — the Semiquincentennial. From the signing of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776, to the present day, this nation has been shaped by its people’s faith, sacrifice, and commitment to liberty and justice for all.

As we mark this extraordinary milestone, we are called not merely to celebrate, but to reflect and pray. The Founders acknowledged dependence upon Divine Providence. It is fitting — and urgent — that we do the same as we enter America’s next chapter.

A Call to Action

America stands at a crossroads. The challenges facing our nation — political division, cultural upheaval, moral drift, and spiritual decline — are not solved by policy alone. They require the intervention of God Himself, invited by the humble, persistent prayers of His people.

We invite you: pray for America. Pray for its leaders. Pray for its families and churches. Pray for the 250 years still to come.

The Proclamation

We, the people of faith across these United States, do hereby covenant together before Almighty God to devote the next forty days to earnest, united prayer for this nation — her leaders, her families, her churches, and her future.

Acknowledging that we are “one nation under God,” we humble ourselves and seek His face. We confess that as a nation we have strayed from His ways. We ask for His mercy, His healing, and His guidance as America marks 250 years of liberty.

We believe that “the effectual fervent prayer of a righteous person availeth much” (James 5:16), and that God still hears the prayers of His people.

Therefore, we pray. For America. For liberty. For righteousness. For the glory of God.
